About me

Hello! I'm Steven Scudari!

I am a computer science major at UC Irvine who is looking for CS work.

Before attending UC Irvine, I worked as a part-time tutor at Pasadena City College. I was able to help my peers while also improving my computer science and teaching skills. I was also the treasurer of She.Codes, a club focused on the inclusion of women in CS.

From both UC Irvine and Pasadena City College, I have learned many computer science skills that have prepared me to tackle almost any field. I am ready to learn something new if I need to, but I am already proficient in C++, Python, Java and more!

In my free time I like to solve puzzles like the Rubik's cube and play video games. They're a big reason why I got into programming! I am also constantly looking for the next CS challenge to take up. Scroll down to see some of the projects that I have done!

Projects

Here are some of my most recent projects. Click on the name to see more!

Attack the Towers!

In this game, made with Kent Phan and Ralph Parin for LA Hacks 2021, you place towers to stop the enemy from advancing to your side. The enemy also places towers based on the position of your units. You will need to upgrade your towers and overwhelm the enemy forces... and the enemy AI!

Made with Unity

Closer Together

This app, made with Rafael Betita for HackUCI 2021, was a response to the separation we felt from others during Covid-19. In this app, you can save contacts to a Firebase server including their basic info but also the next time you want to speak to them. The app will then remind you to keep in touch so that you can feel closer together.

Made with Javascript, React, Grommet, and Firebase

Music Player

Unsatisfied with the standard MP3 players that Windows offered, I set out to make my own. This app allows you to import your songs and will sort them by song order. It gets this info from the metadata stored in MP3 files. The user can then play their songs with a very simple, yet unintrusive, interface.

Made with C++ and the Qt GUI
